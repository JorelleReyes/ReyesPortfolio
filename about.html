Hi, I'm Jorelle


In my free time, I work on various electronics projects, including hand-wired mechanical keyboards,
DIY electronic circuits, and audio systems, among others. Im currently pursuing
Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) at Mapúa University, where I also have an interest
in computer programming, 3D animation, video editing, photo editing, and much more.
Over the years, I've gained experience in repairing gadgets like computers and smartphones.
My curiosity started at age 13, when I began taking things apart to see how they worked inside.


This hands-on exploration has given me a solid understanding of how both electronic and
mechanical devices function, and Im always fascinated by the inner workings of things.
For example, when I see a car, I often imagine its internal mechanics—the movement of pistons
in the engine, how the transmission gears work in tandem to propel the vehicle, and so on.


I also have a deep passion for music. I enjoy listening to a wide range of genres and play several
instruments, including bass guitar, electric guitar, drums, and ukulele. Music is a big part of
my life—it helps me organize my thoughts and influences my mood. Whether Im listening to upbeat tunes
when Im happy or energizing electronic music to power through tasks, it always has a positive effect on me.
